    29 Feb 2024, 00:00

    A star athlete at an Arizona high school is dead after shooting himself in the head on campus Tuesday morning, just moments after revealing his deadly plan on Twitter. 

    Lt. Scott Smith said 18-year-old Marcus Wheeler, a track star attending Corona del Sol High School in Tempe, ended his life after holding a handgun to his head while in an exterior breezeway.

    According to Smith, life-saving efforts at the scene were unsuccessful.

    Scroll down for video 

    Tragic end: Marcus Wheeler, a track and cross country star attending Corona del Sol High School in Tempe, Arizona, ended his life after holding a handgun to his head

    Wheeler, 18, committed suicide in an exterior breezeway next to the gym; no other students were around at the time 

    Emergency: The school was placed on lockdown when authorities learned of reports of a person on campus with a gun

    All classes at the school serving about 3,000 students were canceled a short time after the tragic shooting

    Smith said other students were in class and that none were present when the student shot himself.

    The school was placed on lockdown when authorities learned of reports of a person on campus with a gun.

    Tempe police spokeswoman Molly Enright told the station KPHO that just before 9am, officials at Corona del Sol High contacted the school resource officer saying a person with a gun was spotted near the gym.

    The high school serving about 3,000 students was placed on lockdown as the guard tracked down Marcus Wheeler and attempted to convince him to drop the gun.

    But the 18-year-old senior refused to listen and shot himself in the head.

    All classes were canceled a short time later and students were dismissed for the day at 10.15am.

    Final message: Wheeler, who went by the user name 'TigerHoods' on Twitter, sent a heartbreaking message just before 9am warning of his intent to take his life

    Failed talks: A resource officer tracked down Wheeler and tried to talk him out of killing himself, but the 18-year-old senior refused to listen and pulled the trigger 

    Wheeler was described by those who knew him as a popular student and a standout athlete who ran cross country and track.

    Wheeler, who went by the user name 'TigerHoods' on Twitter, sent a heartbreaking message just before 9am warning of his intent to take his own life.

    'There is going to be a suicide in the school right now,' the 18-year-old boy wrote.

    His penultimate tweet, sent an hour before his death, read: 'No motivation to do anything.'

    As news of the shooting spread, Wheeler's friends took to social media to mourn the beloved student-athleteâ€™s untimely death.

    Tevin Mayfield, who was part of Corona del Sol's Division I state championship track team last season, told AZCentral Wheeler was like a little brother to him and he spoke to him on the phone almost every day.

    Champion: Wheeler was described by those who knew him as a popular student and a standout athlete who ran cross country and track

    Wheeler was considered among the top long-distance runners in the state and was a 2014 Arizona state cross country champion

    'I could have stopped it, if i was in Arizona i would have saved him,' Mayfield, who is currently a freshman at Colorado State, lamented on Twitter.

    The high school track and field news site MileSplit USA revealed Tuesday that Wheeler was considered among the top long-distance runners in the state and was a 2014 Arizona state cross country champion.

    According to the site, the 18-year-old had committed to run for Central Arizona College next year.
