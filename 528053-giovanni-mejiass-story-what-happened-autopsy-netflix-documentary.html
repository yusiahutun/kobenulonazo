    Amidst numerous murder cases in the United States, the Giovanni Mejias tragedy got unparalleled attention. Despite it happening in 2017, the story is still a focal point of interest, with people searching for more information about what transpired.

    Giovanni Mejias' story

    On June 13 2017, the police were called at around 10:45 am to a report of a child (Giovanni Mejias) not responsive inside a home on North Green Meadow Street. Paramedics took the child to Helen DeVos Children's Hospital, where he was pronounced dead.

    According to Elis Ortiz, he was at home with seven children that day; three were his, three were his girlfriend's (including Mejias), and one was his niece. Ortiz claimed when he woke up, he found Giovanni Mejias on the kitchen floor, not breathing with serious stomach bloating. He ordered one of the other children to call 911 as he performed CPR on Mejias.

    Giovanni Mejias autopsy

    The medical examiner, Dr. David, later ruled Giovanni Mejias's death a murder from fatal abdomen injuries, adding that he had several older injuries like fractured ribs and bruising. He described Mejias' injuries as "remarkable" based on the location and number of injuries.

    Dr. David stated the injuries, which were at different healing levels, were a sign of abuse by an adult, and this led him to conclude the child's death was murder.

    Who is Elis Ortiz?

    Elis Ortiz is Giovanni Mejias' father, although not his birth father. The couple began dating in December 2016 and moved in together in February 2017. They lived with seven children; three of the kids were Ortiz's, three were Hernandez's, and one was Ortiz's niece.

    What did Giovanni Mejias' mother say about Elis Ortiz in court?

    Giovanni Mejias' mother, Hernandez, strongly defended her boyfriend, Elis Ortiz. In her testimony during a preliminary Grand Rapids District Court hearing, Hernandez claimed that Ortiz had a good heart and loved children. She added that Giovanni Mejias loved Ortiz and wanted to be just like him.

    Was Elis Nelson Ortiz-Nieves released?

    No, Elis Nelson Ortiz-Nieves was sentenced to mandatory life in prison without the parole possibility for felony murder. He was also sentenced to between 80 and 150 years for first-degree child abuse.
