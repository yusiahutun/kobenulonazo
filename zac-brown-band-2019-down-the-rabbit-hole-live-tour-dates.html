    Zac Brown Band announced new music and a new tour on Friday morning (Nov. 9). The "Chicken Fried" group will play arenas and amphitheaters nationwide beginning in March.

    Fifteen new tour dates were revealed in a press release, starting with a March 1 stop in Tulsa, Okla., and ending in Peoria, Ill. on April 27. The dates are primarily in more moderate climates and heavily focused on the southeast. The Down the Rabbit Hole Live Tour does not have an opening act as of now.

    The eight-piece band spent 2018 touring Major League Baseball stadiums including Fenway Park, where they've sold out nine consecutive shows. Zac Brown Band live at Fenway is becoming almost as much of a tradition as Kenny Chesney at Gillette Stadium in Boston (Foxborough, Mass.).

    Tickets will go on sale to the general public on Nov. 16, but fans who received refunds when the group was forced to cancel shows due to Hurricane Florence will get a priority pre-sale opportunity for shows in Raleigh, Charlotte and Virginia Beach starting on Nov. 14.

    See the full list of Zac Brown Band Down the Rabbit Hole Live tour dates below. Additionally, the group released a new single called "Someone I Used to Know," co-written by Shawn Mendes.
