    How do you lead an opening prayer?

    Leading an opening prayer can be a meaningful and rewarding experience, but it can also feel intimidating or unfamiliar if you're new to it. Below is a guide on how to start a prayer.

    • Take a few moments before the prayer to centre yourself and connect with the divine. You might want to read through the prayer ahead of time or even write your own prayer if you feel comfortable doing so.
    • Express your gratitude to God for the blessings in your life, and offer words of praise for God's goodness, love, and power. You might also reflect on a specific aspect of God's character or a recent experience that has inspired you.
    • Recognize God's presence and ask for His guidance and blessing. You might say something like, "We gather in your presence, O God, and ask that you be with us as we pray."
    • Ask God for the things that you need or want, whether it be healing, guidance, forgiveness, or strength. You might say something like, "We ask for your help and guidance, Lord, as we face the challenges of the day."
    • Include prayers for others, whether it be for their health, safety, comfort, or well-being. This can help to cultivate a sense of compassion and connectedness.
