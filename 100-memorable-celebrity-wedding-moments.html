    This is the moment we've all been waiting for: The first photos from George Clooney and Amal Alamuddin’s wedding ceremony have arrived!

    As one would imagine, the couple looked beyond gorgeous as they tied the knot at Aman Canal Grande Venice in Italy on Sept. 27. They appear on the cover of this week's People magazine (and Hello! internationally), which includes 25 photos from their wedding album. Matt Damon, Emily Blunt, and John Krasinski were among the approximately 100 guests who attended the private service (see their fashions here). Clooney, 53, told People that marriage to London barrister Alamuddin, 36, "feels pretty damn great."

    So what exactly did the bride and groom wear for the big day? Alamuddin dazzled in an exquisite custom-made Oscar de la Renta wedding gown of French lace that featured a floor-brushing train and hand-embroidered pearls and diamanté accents. A long, elegant veil graced her hair and swept down to the ground.

    [wrn_tweet title="It's Love!"]Amal Alamuddin wore this exquisite Oscar de la Renta wedding dress to marry George Clooney![/wrn_tweet]

    Clooney also opted for classic style and suited up in a black tuxedo of ultra-fine wool and cashmere that was created by Giorgio Armani as part of the Made to Measure collection. He also wore a black bow tie, black brushed-leather oxfords, People reports. But perhaps the most significant pieces Clooney donned for the momentous occasion are his cufflinks, which were inscribed with "George" in Arabic—and were given to him by Alamuddin!

    Amal Alamuddin & George Clooney

    When Amal Alamuddin and George Clooney tied the knot in Venice, Italy, the bride wore an exquisite Oscar de la Renta gown that featured hand-embroidered pearls and diamanté accents. The groom donned a Giorgio Armani tuxedo.

    Neil Patrick Harris & David Burtka

    The two exchanged vows in a lavish Italian ceremony. Elton John was on hand to perform a few love songs and stayed to introduce the couple's first dance.

    Angelina Jolie & Brad Pitt

    When the superstars finally tied the knot at Château Miraval their estate in Correns, France, Jolie donned a custom Versace gown and veil that featured sewed-on drawings by the children.

    Ellen DeGeneres & Portia de Rossi

    Adding a personal touch to their small gathering, the couple wed at their L.A. home. "The love we felt from the people surrounding us, and the love we have for each other, made this the most beautiful and emotional day," de Rossi told People. "And one that we'll never forget."

    Olivia Palermo & Johannes Huebl

    The couple looked stylishly stunning at their wedding ceremony in Bedford, New York. For the intimate occasion, Palermo donned a three-piece Carolina Herrera ensemble, Manolo Blahnik pumps, and Alexandra Mor earrings, while Huebl opted for a Marc Anthony Hamburg suit and shirt, a Charvet tie, and Etro pocket square.

    Eve & Maximillion Cooper

    Eve wore a stunning beaded Alice Temperley dress when she tied the knot with Maximillion Cooper. The couple met at the 2010 Gumball 3000 motor rally and got married on June 13, 2014, in Ibiza, Spain.

    Kim Kardashian & Kanye West

    Kim Kardashian stunned in a custom-made Givenchy haute couture gown designed by Riccardo Tisci she tied the knot with Kanye West at the Forte Di Belvedere in Florence, Italy, in front of a 200-person crowd of close family and friends.

    Elton John & David Furnish

    David Furnish gave a thumbs up to his union after his civil partnership ceremony with Elton John. The longtime couple scheduled their wedding for the first day that same-sex couples could legally marry in England.

    Kaley Cuoco & Ryan Sweeting

    The Big Bang Theory's Kaley Cuoco and tennis star Ryan Sweeting said "I do" on New Year's Eve in Southern California. For the lavish, love-filled event, Cuoco chose a strapless, pastel pink Vera Wang gown that she styled with single strand of pearls.

    Keira Knightley & James Righton

    Talk about a low-maintenance! In a small ceremony in Provence, France, Keira Knightley said "I do" to fiancé James Righton in a no-fuss strapless tulle dress that she paired with a Chanel jacket, ballet flats and a crown of flowers.

    Jessica Biel & Justin Timberlake

    Is pink the new white? Jessica Biel exchanged vows in a custom petal-pink Giambattista Valli Haute Couture gown. The celebrations weren’t restricted to one night-it continued at the Borgo Egnazia resort (located in southern Italy) for an entire week.

    Crissy Teigen & John Legend

    It's no secret that Chrissy Teigen is a fan of Vera Wang's work. Just four days after sitting front row at the designer’s spring/summer 2014 show, the model tied the knot with singer John Legend in not one, but three Vera Wang dresses.

    Catherine Middleton

    Her title didn’t officially change until she said “I do,” but the bride went from Kate Middleton to the Duchess of Cambridge in our eyes the second we saw her dressed in Alexander McQueen.

    Nicole Kidman

    The private star couldn’t keep her joy under wraps as she arrived at Sydney’s Cardinal Cerretti Memorial Chapel for her 2006 wedding.

    Alicia Keys & Swizz Beatz

    Swizz Beatz stole a kiss from his Greek goddess wife when they wed in 2010 on the Mediterranean Sea.

    Barack & Michelle Obama

    After dating for three years, Michelle and Barack tied the knot on October 3, 1992. Of their vows, Michelle has said, “Barack didn’t pledge riches, only a life that would be interesting. On that promise he delivered.”

    A Royal Pageboy

    He made the ladies swoon in navy at his brother’s 2011 wedding, but it doesn’t get much cuter than the white pageboy uniform Prince Harry wore to his uncle’s 1989 affair.

    Prince Rainier III & Grace Kelly

    After their afternoon nuptials on April 19, 1956, guests lunched al fresco and finished their meal with a traditional cake adorned with cherubs.

    Gavin Rossdale & Gwen Stefani

    After a London bash, the beaming twosome cruised into the sunset before renewing their vows before friends and family in L.A.

    Mick Jagger

    True to form, Mick Jagger partied like a rock star with his own bottle of champagne after tying the knot in St. Tropez.

    Jacqueline Kennedy

    The undivided attention of 15 handsome men, including her husband-to-be and his Kennedy kin, would make even a woman as poised as Jacqueline Kennedy blush.

    Lady Gaga

    The unpredictable singer-songwriter blindsided us when she shed her wild on-stage costumes for a blush-pink versatile Twobirds wrap dress (it can be worn 15 ways) when she assumed her bridesmaid role at BFF Bo O’Connor’s wedding in Cabo San Lucas, Mexico.

    Anne Hathaway & Adam Shulman

    Actress Anne Hathaway wed Adam Shulman in a breathtaking custom-made off-the-shoulder ivory silk and d'esprit tulle gown by designer Valentino Garavani. But what makes this creation extraordinary is the train, which was hand-painted with shades of pink and embroidered with satin flowers.

    Francois-Henri & Salma Hayek-Pinault

    The couple first wed in a surprise ceremony at the 6th Arrondisement City Hall in Paris on Valentine's Day 2009. Two months later, they married again at Venice's opera house, Teatro La Fenice, in front of a star-studded audience, which included Penelope Cruz and Charlize Theron.

    Joe DiMaggio & Marilyn Monroe

    The larger-than-life stars chose a decidedly low-key venue: San Francisco’s City Hall. The morning headline’s said it all: “DiMaggio Signs With a New Manager.”

    Pippa Middleton

    The maid of honor stole the show the second she stepped out of her carriage. We wonder why.

    Ivanka Trump

    Donald's daughter was reportedly inspired by Grace Kelly's classic gown: She converted to Judaism in order to marry Jared Kushner in 2009, and required a slightly more covered-up style. Vera Wang was happy to oblige. "I seized on the chance to do a dress that wasn't naked," the designer said at the time.

    John Lennon & Yoko Ono

    The minimalist couple boarded a private plane in Gibraltar with few personal items besides the folded marriage certificate in John Lennon’s hand.

    Elvis & Priscilla Presley

    Viva Las Vegas! The lovebirds celebrated their May 1967 nuptials with a six-tier design adorned with romantic red roses.

    Lauren Bacall & Humphrey Bogart

    In keeping with their intimate farmhouse wedding, the on and offscreen duo chose a rustic charmer of a cake and (for the first bite) skipped the cutlery.

    Bill & Chelsea Clinton

    When Chelsea Clinton said “I do” at Rhinebeck’s Astor Court Estate in 2010, her father was there to walk her down the very long and picturesque aisle.

    Mike Todd & Elizabeth Taylor

    Elizabeth Taylor’s parents and brother looked on as she signed the register at her 1957 wedding to Mike Todd.

    Princess Diana

    Few images from the 1981 royal wedding are as iconic Princess Diana’s train, all 25 feet of it!

    Charlize Theron

    On her way to Francois-Henri and Salma Hayek-Pinault’s Venetian wedding, Charlize Theron wowed in a floor-grazing Dior halter dress. On the way out she caught our eye for other reasons-the gray gown had seemingly transformed into a mini.

    Robert Downey Jr. & Susan Levin

    At Robert Downey Jr. and Susan Levin’s 2005 wedding, the couple wed under a gazebo as guests like Billy Joel and Sting watched from white wooden benches. In keeping with the relaxed tone, the groom wore sneakers.

    Henry & Jenna Bush Hager

    The couple chose the Ultimate Cheesecake Bakery’s charming design for their Texas ranch wedding. The tres leches confection was topped with layers of whipped cream and periwinkle blossoms.

    Drew Barrymore & Will Kopelman

    The actress told us she wanted "something tasteful, something timeless." There’s nothing more tasteful and timeless than a custom Chanel Couture gown. Drew Barrymore married Will Kopelman in a pleated muslin and organza gown embroidered with tulle flowers, designed by the one and only Karl Lagerfeld.

    Paul McCartney

    One perk of being a Beatle? Having Sir Paul McCartney perform at your wedding. The Brit tickled the ivories at Ringo Starr’s 1981 reception at London’s Club Rags.

    Princess Madeleine of Sweden

    Valentino Garavani came out of retirement to sketch a wedding dress to royally befit a princess-and that he did, creating a dress made with silk organdies and ivory Chantilly lace with a train that measured four meters long and a veil that measured six. "(Princess Madeleine) is a very lovely girl. She is modern, fun, full of energy and enthusiasm, and she is so beautiful! It has been a pleasure and an honor," he said in a statement.

    Danny Moder & Julia Roberts

    The private couple shared an intimate embrace at their Fourth of July Wedding. The small ceremony took place at Julia Roberts’s New Mexico Ranch.

    Rihanna

    Though she’s know for her more outrageous getups, Rihanna arrived in subdued black for her manager’s 2008 Italian wedding.

    Mel Ferrer & Audrey Hepburn

    The British actress created her own fairytale when she wed Mel Ferrer in a romantic woodland wedding.

    Bart Freundlich & Julianne Moore

    Julianne Moore and Bart Freundlich made it a family affair when they tied the knot in an intimate N.Y.C. ceremony. The bride arrived carrying the couple’s infant daughter Liv, while the groom walked hand-in-hand with their son Caleb.

    Tom Hanks & Rita Wilson

    They’ve teamed up many times on screen, but we’ll bet this was their favorite collaboration. The happy couple married at Rex’s in 1988 and have been smiling since.

    Jackie Titone & Adam Sandler

    Jackie Titone embraced her husband-to-be’s culture before the wedding by converting to Judaism and at the wedding by clipping Adam Sandler’s yarmulke to his head.

    Rachel Bilson

    It's a good thing Josh Schwartz has keen ears. Otherwise, the creator and executive producer of Gossip Girl may not have overheard Rachel Bilson trying to set up her BFF, Jill Stonerock, with her O.C. co-star Benjamin McKenzie. "I said, 'What about me? Ben doesn't need help,'" says Schwartz. At the couple’s 2008 Los Olivos wedding the bridesmaids wore earthy green Brian Reyes designs.

    Paul Newman & Joanne Woodward

    Though their wedding was in Las Vegas, their cake was anything but flashy. The newlyweds cut into a modest three-tier dessert.

    Jerry & Jessica Seinfeld

    Looks like this comedian’s not the one with jokes. Jerry Seinfeld shared a laugh with his new wife after their 2000 wedding in New York, naturally.

    Jerry O'Connell & Rebecca Romijn

    "The wedding was about celebrating with out friends and having fun-not about being stressed," says Romijn, who got a boost from O'Connell, his brother, Charlie, designer Cynthia Rowley and other friends.

    Natalie Portman

    The Rodarte sisters designed Natalie Portman’s unforgettable Black Swan costumes and the gown she wore to accept her Oscar. So it made sense that the award-winning actress turned to the duo again to create for another major moment: Her nuptials to Benjamin Millepied.

    John Travolta

    John Travolta strutted his stuff down the aisle at his brother’s 1980 affair.

    Celine Dion

    Never one to shy from the spotlight, Celine Dion sparkled like the Las Vegas strip when she renewed her vows to Rene Angelil at Ceasar’s Palace.

    Tricia Nixon

    After waltzing with her groom, Tricia Nixon joined her father on the White House dance floor.

    Mick & Bianca Jagger

    St. Tropez had a rock and roll moment when The Rolling Stones’ frontman tied the knot with his bride at a small fisherman’s church.

    Britney Spears

    When Britney Spears’s assistant tied the knot, the pop princess reigned as the Maid of Honor in a mint halter gown.

    Jennifer Nicholson

    On screen he’s a legend, but to his daughter Jack Nicholson’s just dad. The doting father escorted Jennifer Nicholson down the aisle at her 1997 Hotel Bel Air wedding.

    Shenae Grimes & Josh Beech

    The 90210 actress defied tradition, eschewing the usual white, and instead, walked down the aisle in a black Vera Wang wedding dress.

    Ashley Olsen

    When her stylist, Estee Stanley, wed in 2008, Ashley Olsen went incognito while adding flair to her bridesmaid ensemble.

    Prince Albert II & Charlene Wittstock

    Following in the tradition of his parents, Prince Albert II of Monaco served a towering cake at his wedding to Charlene Wittstock. The massive five-tier confection featured a white and pink floral motif and a cornucopia cake topper.

    Lauren Bacall

    After a brief ceremony that lasted only 3 minutes, Lauren Bacall tossed her bouquet into the small crowd of guests waiting on the landing.

    Jayne Mansfield

    True to form, the smoldering star flaunted her figure in a lace mermaid gown at her 1958 wedding. The centerfold got a boost while making her way down the aisle in her restrictive design.

    Halle Berry

    Six years ago, the Oscar-winning actress told InStyle she’d never get married again. But on July 12, 2013, Halle Berry surprised us (and perhaps herself) when she wed French actor and beau Olivier Martinez at the Chateau des Conde in Vallery, France several months before her expected due date.

    Naomi Watts

    Naomi Watts broke the wedding taboo and wore a lacey LWD to her friend’s South Wales nuptials.

    Conan O’Brien & Liza Powel

    When it rains it pours, fortunately the late night host was prepared for the Seattle weather and brought a well-coordinated black and white umbrella.

    Catherine Middleton

    Even duchesses repeat outfits! When Catherine Middleton and Prince William attended the wedding of Zara Phillips and Mike Tindall in Scotland the Duchess of Cambridge wore an ivory brocade coat by Jane Troughton buttoned up over a lace dress. This is the same combo she wore for the wedding of Harry Lopes and Laura Parker Bowles, daughter of Duchess of Cornwall Camilla Bowles, in 2006.

    Elvis & Priscilla Presley

    He may have been accustomed to girls throwing themselves at him, but this heartthrob seemed caught off guard when he and his bride were showered with confetti.

    Princess Diana

    While preparing for her larger-than-life moment, The Princess of Wales took an intimate moment to comfort her nervous young attendant, Miss Clementine Hambro.

    Prince Rainier III of Monaco & Grace Kelly

    The American actress became a princess when Prince Rainier III paired Grace Kelly’s 12-carat engagement ring with a wedding band.

    Geoffrey Arend & Christina Hendricks

    The newlyweds chose “Origin of Love,” from the 2001 film Hedwig and the Angry Inch, for their first dance. “We made our friends get on the dance floor with us halfway through,” says Hendricks.

    Sting & Trudie Styler

    The couple, now married 19 years, celebrated their union by blowing kisses at the camera.

    Frank Sinatra & Mia Farrow

    The couple kept it short and sweet, much like the bride’s famous pixie, at their intimate Las Vegas wedding.

    Brian Austin Green & Megan Fox

    The longtime couple tied the knot Hawaiian style when a local priest married them in an intimate Kona ceremony.

    Robert Wagner & Natalie Wood

    In 1957 the superstar couple wed in Scottsdale Arizona. Though they divorced four years later, they got another chance at a first kiss when they wed again in 1972.

    Temperley Bridal's Kate Middleton-Inspired Dress

    Fans and designers alike are taking style cues from the Duchess of Cambridge. In 2012, Temperley Bridal introduced a bridal version of Kate Middleton’s red carpet premiere look. Who wouldn’t want to look like royalty?

    A Very Kennedy Bridal Party

    Caroline Kennedy and Maria Shriver wore matching sun hats to the June wedding of their cousin Courtney.

    Ryan Sutter & Trista Rehn

    Since they had fallen in love on TV, The Bachelorette couple fittingly tied the knot for the world to see. The reality show duo filmed a three-part special that aired on ABC following their 2003 wedding.

    Will & Jada Pinkett Smith

    This inseparable couple even walked down the aisle together at their Baltimore wedding. The duo exchanged personalized vows before dancing into the wee hours. On the playlist: "Gettin' Jiggy Wit It!"

    Marcia Cross

    Seven flower girls-Cross's niece and the daughters of her closest friends, clad in white Reem Acra dresses-carried silk douppioni baskets and tiny bouquets down the aisle.

    Blake Lively & Ryan Reynolds

    Blake Lively and Ryan Reynolds’ ceremony in South Carolina was so private and secretive, it took everyone (outside of friends and family) by complete surprise. What's more, the duo ensured wedding bliss after they sought help from Martha Stewart who partook in the planning.

    Peter Hermann & Mariska Hargitay

    Very Different Cakes in L.A. created the couple's ornate confection. A bright orange, magenta and green color palette set a festive tone.

    Tony Walton & Julie Andrews

    The Mary Poppins star kept it sweet and PG with a peck on the cheek at her 1959 wedding.

    Liza Minelli & Sammy Davis Jr.

    Who other than Liza Minelli could share the stage with Sammy Davis Jr. at their wedding? That’s exactly what the entertainer did at her 1986 affair.

    Caleb Followill & Lily Aldridge

    The Victoria’s Secret Angel married Kings of Leon frontman Caleb Followill during an intimate ceremony at the San Ysidro Ranch in Montecito, California. “It was a perfect day,” Followill said.

    Kevin Costner & Christine Baumgartner

    Nothing says rustic Colorado wedding like a celebratory paddle. Kevin Costner and his bride took an intimate canoe ride after their Aspen ranch nuptials.

    Roger Vadim & Brigitte Bardot

    Far from the blonde bombshell she later portrayed on film, the French actress was a demure brunette when she said “I do,” to her film director husband.

    Dean McDermott & Tori Spelling

    The couple recorded their private ceremony so they could later share it at a larger party celebrating the nuptials. "We wanted people to feel they were a part of it," Spelling says. The footage of the couple's week in Fiji, shot on Super-8 film, was made into a 40-minute silent movie by Fifty Foot Films of L.A. and N.Y.C., and projected onto a wall at the reception.

    Dame Judi Dench

    Before she was a Dame, Judi Dench was a blushing bride at her 1971 Hampstead London wedding.

    A Beatles Sandwich

    Paul McCartney added an honorary member to the band at George Harrison and Patti Boyd’s wedding.

    Lisa Ling

    The guest list at Lisa Ling’s “Asian chic” affair included Connie Chung, Kelly Hu and two of the bride’s most fashionable pals, Patricia Herrera and a pre-Bravo Rachel Zoe.

    Timbaland's DJ Session

    The music never stopped at the music mogul’s 2008 wedding-a first dance to Al Green's "Love and Happiness," performances from jazz musician Mike Phillips and gospel singer J Moss, and Timbaland himself behind the turntables. "It was the best time I've had in my life," said the groom.

    Kate Bosworth & Michael Polish

    The takeaway here: When you can't decide on a wedding band, get two. With the help of the team at Ocappi, Kate Bosworth's wish was granted. The first 18-baguette-diamond encrusted band was designed to complement her engagement ring. As for the second one, "Michael mentioned that maybe we should do something with 12 stones representing each month of the year," Ocappi creative director Haki Abehsera told us. "That's where we decided not to be subtle and how ring number two was created."

    Wolfgang Puck & Gelila Assefa

    Celebrity chef Wolfgang Puck and his bride were escorted from their Capri ceremony to the reception-which was held at the Grand Hotel Quisisana-by Scialapopolo, a local folk-dance troupe.

    Nat King Cole & Maria Hawkins Ellington

    The Unforgettable singer created a memorable moment when he escorted his jazz singer bride onto the floor at their Harlem wedding.

    Max Handelman & Elizabeth Banks

    Hansen's Cakes created the three-tier chocolate-and-vanilla cake, garnished with fresh orchids. The wedding favors? Each of their 180 guests was offered a second sweet ending-a box of Krispy Kreme doughnuts.

    Bobby Flay & Stephanie March

    Food Network’s Bobby Flay cooked up a peck for his SVU bride at their 2005 N.Y.C. affair. For the second course, the couple dined with friends and family at celebrity chef Daniel Boulud’s restaurant, Daniel.

    Roger Federer & Mirka Vavrinec

    Guests served up a modern alternative to rice when tennis star Roger Federer emerged from the church after his 2009 wedding.

    Lake Bell & Scott Campbell

    Marchesa designer Georgina Champan once told us she loves dressing all brides, "but personally, my favorites are those who are risk-takers, who aren't afraid to push boundaries,” which pretty much makes Lake Bell "the one." Her unconventional Marchesa creation had a turtleneck, tiers and intricate beading all over.

    Mike Tindall & Zara Phillips

    Zara Phillips, granddaughter of Queen Elizabeth, married her rugby player beau Mike Tindall on Saturday, July 30th at Canongate Kirk in Edinburgh, Scotland. The bride chose a white silk and satin dress with a chevron pleated corseted bodice by British couturier Stewart Parvin, a favorite of the Queen.

    Paul Anderson & Milla Jovovich

    Her onscreen characters are often tough, but for her 2009 Beverly Hills wedding the former model showed her softer side in an ethereal gown and wide grin.

    100 of 100

    Barry Diller & Diane von Furstenberg

    In 2000, the fashionable couple married in a small ceremony at New York’s City Hall.
