    Grab a gilet, whatever the season - it’ll be your wardrobe’s hardest working addition

      We've reached January, and that means spring is on its way (yes, we've gone there). So if you're shopping for outerwear that will take you through to next season, may we introduce the gilet. 

      A lightweight but cosy choice, it's ideal for layering over tees, dresses, sweaters and knits. These versatile gilets – also known as puffer vests in the US – are always one of our wardrobe's hardest-working pieces.

      We love a chic black gilet, or other classic hues likes navy and off-white. A fleece or borg fabric will keep out the cold, while a sporty style or puffer gilet will add some sports luxe to your closet.

      The best gilets at a glance

      The sporty gilet: The North Face 1996 Retro Nuptse Down Puffer Gilet, £250

      The affordable gilet: M&S Borg Textured Funnel Neck Gilet, £29.50

      The designer gilet: Canada Goose Freestyle Quilted Shell Down Vest, £575

      The celebrity-approved gilet: Uniqlo Ultra Lightweight Down Vest, £39.90

      Royals and celebrities wearing gilets

      Royals and celebrities love them too. Princess Kate loves a luxury quilted gilet for outdoor playdates and casual engagements. She prefers a khaki or brown-hue, as worn in 2021 on a royal visit to Scotland.

      A-List stars like Hailey Bieber choose a gilet as an evening jacket; she perfectly demonstrates how cool it can look over a smart dress, adding a laidback luxe look.

      How to wear a gilet

      Layering over jeans and trousers is a given, but we're into the aesthetic of gilets over dresses too. Try wearing over a denim jacket, shearling coat or, on those chilly summer days, a snuggly sweatshirt. We love the look of wearing a bright gilet with black, or even matching your gilet to your outfit - one colour for all. And if a padded gilet isn't for you, don't dismiss a quilted, faux fur or teddy version.

      How we chose the best gilets

      • Price: We've searched high and low for gilets to match all budgets, from £29.50 for a high street gilet to £575 for a designer buy.
      • Variety: Not all gilets are made equal. The puffer style is the most traditional but don't dismiss the lighter weight teddy or fleece gilets, or the luxe-looking faux-fur sleeveless vests.
      • Practicality: Where possible, we've chosen gilets with practical elements like pockets and waterproof materials.
      • Trusted brands: All of the labels we've chosen are trusted brands the HELLO! team swear by; we've not tried on all of the products but the brands are some of our favourites, so quality should be high.
