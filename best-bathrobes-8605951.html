    Robes for most are often like a giant security blanket. After a long day, don’t we all just want to come home and be enveloped by something that feels totally cozy? The best robes offer this extreme comfort factor and more. Whether a plush and warm option is on your mind, or something lightweight that will transcend the seasons is more your speed, finding the right robe has never been easier. We asked the hard questions and put dozens (38) to the test to find the best robes in every category and material imaginable.

    We asked our real-world testers to assess the robes’ quality by describing their softness, comfort, and durability after wearing and washing them regularly over the testing period. Was the material heavy or lightweight? Was it easy to overheat, or was it not warm enough? In terms of function, did the robe stay tied when on, or did it have any features like pockets or a hood that seemed particularly useful? 22 robes made the cut for this roundup, including warm flannels for the coldest of winter days, terry cloth alternatives to keep you dry post-shower, and short, silky styles to satisfy any fashion lover's itch for something luxurious.

    Best Overall

    Parachute Waffle Robe

    Parachute

    What We Love: The Turkish cotton material is Oeko-Tex certified, making it a sustainably made option.

    What We Don’t Love: Some customers found it tore or snagged easily.

    Parachute’s Short Waffle robe is a year-round option that feels like you’re throwing on your favorite cardigan. Made from soft, Turkish cotton, the textured waffle knit is a signature for high-end spa robes — however, you don’t have to go anywhere to indulge in the luxurious nature of this option. This waffle robe can be worn over and over, in the comfort of your own home.

    The material is extremely lightweight and made from cotton, which is notoriously breathable, so overheating is never a concern. The wider, bell sleeves are loose and oversized, as is the overall silhouette in general, adding the extra ability to be layered with ease — if you’d like to wear pajamas underneath and more. Hitting just above the knee, the fit is not too short or too long — it simply rests in the middle.

    Although there was no internal extra tie, we found that the sash stayed firmly tied and never unraveled. The two side pockets are deep enough to stash a cell phone and more, should you be wearing the garment around the house doing chores, or sneaking out to a backyard hot tub during the colder months for a warm plunge. A towel hook makes hanging it up post-use a breeze, and it’s a machine-washable item that is very low maintenance. Classic, comfortable, and lightweight — what more can you ask for?

    Material: Turkish cotton | Size: XS–XL | Colors: 3

    Best Overall, Runner-Up

    Cuyana Washable Charmeuse Robe

    Cuyana

    What We Love: Although made from a silk fabric, it doesn’t require dry cleaning.

    What We Don’t Love: The brand tends to run large, rather than true to size.

    Cuyana’s Washable Charmeuse Robe brings the luxury of silk to a simple robe design, without the unnecessary headache of a “dry clean only” tag. When a garment is meant to be worn over and over, silk usually becomes too bothersome, due to its restrictive care instructions. Thankfully, Cuyana found a silky charmeuse that is as easy to wear often as it is to clean regularly when necessary.

    The soft, breathable fabric won’t cause irritation either, for those with sensitive skin. The lightweight nature of the silk fabric makes it pleasant to wear during spring, summer, and fall seasons (but an alternative option might be needed during the winter for those looking for extra warmth in colder climates). The relaxed silhouette also adds an airy quality to the overall experience that gives it a breezy, not-too-clingy feel.

    One of the factors that we love, when it comes to this wrap design, is that the ties stayed securely snug during testing. Often with silkier style robes, there is a tendency for the sash to loosen and fall, but this one stayed firmly tied. There is also an additional internal tie for extra security, giving it more of a defined waistline look. Elegant and functional, this is a winner in our book.

    Material: Charmeuse silk | Size: XS–XL | Colors: 5

    What to Keep in Mind

    • Sizing and Fit: “You never want a robe to be snug, this is the time for cozy comfort,” says bra specialist Jenny Altman. “Size up to make sure the robe will fit well even after its first wash, and after many washes in years to come.” She also recommends making sure the hem length is best for you, as well as the arm length. “I always prefer robes that are at least knee-length and long sleeve, all year round (bonus points for a cozy lining, hood, and pockets!),” she adds. Steward always looks for size-inclusivity. “I only care about oversized robes or size up robes from my usual size because robes are meant to be comfortable,” she says. “A good robe needs better sizing, [and to not be] tight in areas where bodies are usually different, like the arms and waist.”
    • Fabric: “The most important function of a robe is warmth and comfort, so I prioritize this above all else,” says Maia James, founder of non-toxic product review site Gimme the Good Stuff. “Depending on the season, where you live, and whether you run hot or cold will determine the best fabric for you, but my favorite robes are made of light, soft, cotton.” Altman appreciates the different weights for the seasons as well. “I love light, crisp cotton for summer, as much as a heavier, fleece fabric for winter,” she explains. However, for all-year wear, she recommends cotton muslin. “It keeps you cool and warm when you need it, plus muslin always feels soft, you don't need to break it in to feel cozy,” she notes. Steward also pays attention to the fabric. Her personal favorites include mohair and cotton jersey for the ultimate in comfort.
    • Ethical Responsibility: “If possible, the robe you buy should have a low impact on the health of the planet and be made in a socially responsible production facility,” James says. “Whenever possible, I prioritize brands that use organic cotton to make their robes, or that have other ethical fashion certifications, like B Corp.” Steward recommends buying from small brands that cut and sew themselves. “There is just more love that goes into it,” she says.
    • Price: “Price is important to most people when robe shopping because it can be a significant investment, especially if you're looking for a high-quality robe,” James says. “But you do not need to spend a ton of money to buy a really great robe — in fact, most of my favorites are less than $100.” Altman agrees that you don't need to spend a fortune on a good robe, but good fabrics do cost. She likes to view investing in one with a cost-per-wear mindset. “You'll likely be wearing this robe for [five years or more], so spending over $100 is not a bad idea since you'll get a lot of use out of this piece,” Altman says. She also reminds us that robes make the best gifts since they are easy to fit and forgiving, so she recommends telling your family which robe you’d appreciate most for a giftable moment.

    Your Questions, Answered

    What makes a robe great?

    “A great robe [feels] like a little indulgence every time you reach for it,” says James. “The best robes provide the right amount of warmth for the season, but they won't be bulky and get in the way when you want to make your coffee or wash your face or whatever else you need to do while wearing it.” Often, the best robes are all about the way they make you feel. “A robe has to really envelop you and make you feel comforted, like a big hug,” Altman says. “I know a robe is good if my daughter tries taking it from me — all the good ones end up in her bathroom.” Steward prioritizes comfort and features when classifying a great robe. “Robes that have lots of space and pockets are my favorite, as a big girl who is always carrying tons of stuff around my house back and forth 24/7,” she says.

    How do you recommend caring for robes?

    “If your robe is a quality fabric, it should be easy to care for,” Altman explains. “The best robes are pre-shrunk so you can put it in the washer and the dryer.” She notes that if your robe is a delicate fabric — like silk or satin — skip the dryer, which breaks down the fabric. “Sturdy cottons are easy. Treat them like a towel,” she adds.

    James’ approach is a little less delicate. “I am sure there are special ways you can or should wash and dry your robes, but honestly, I just toss them in the washer and dryer — and they've held up fine.” She explains that robes are supposed to be easy and comfortable and having to care for a robe in some special way would defeat the purpose. Steward believes that washing is a personal preference, but she does mention she air dries. However, we recommend checking the care labels for those you purchase before buying or washing.
