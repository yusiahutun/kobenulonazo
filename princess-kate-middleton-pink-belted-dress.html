    12 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Catherine, Princess of Wales, stepped out today for another charitable engagement close to her heart.

    The royal officially opened the Young V&A museum of childhood this morning in London. The museum underwent a major revamp to include not only a major collection of archives that capture and record childhood culture and design, but also spaces where “children, young people and families can imagine, play and design.” The charity also offers programs in different culture sectors and hosts events for both kids of all ages, teachers, and adults.

    The Young V&A is a part of the Victoria and Albert Museum, the United Kingdom’s leading museum of art and design.

    Chris Jackson//Getty Images

    Princess Kate—who continues to focus her work as a royal on early development and child care initiatives—was pictured touring the museum with a group of schoolchildren from Globe Primary School.

    “Our children are super keen to talk to the Princess of Wales during her visit @young_vam,” the school shared on Twitter. “This is not a typical Wednesday morning for them!”

    WPA Pool//Getty Images

    For the occasion, the princess wore a dreamy summer dress: a breezy blush midi by London designer Beulah with a built-in belt, a high collar, short sleeves puffed at the shoulders, and dainty buttons at the center. She paired it with white pointed-toe pumps and diamond drop earrings with a flower design. Her hair was styled in relaxed waves, and she wore rosy makeup.

    Kate first wore the lovely dress to Wimbledon in 2021.
