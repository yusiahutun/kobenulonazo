    Each month we will be shining a light on different dog breeds in our series, Dog Breed of the Month. Next up, we look at the gentle and kind Flat-Coated Retrievers.

    Originating from England, the Flat-Coated Retriever (also known as the Wavy-Coated Retriever) is best known for its straight, moderately long coat that protects him from all types of weather, water, and ground cover. They usually have black or liver-coloured fur.

    "Flat-Coated Retrievers are a large medium-coat breed which have a lifespan of under 10 years. They used to be known as the Wavy Coated Retriever, and were previously popular as working gundogs in the late 1800s," Shauna Spooner, vet nurse at the PDSA, tells Country Living. "They are gentle and kind, but have high energy levels. They are slow to mature, and can display puppy-like behaviour into old age!"

    Keep reading for everything you need to know about flat-coated retrievers:

    What behaviour does a Flat-Coated Retriever have?

    In the company of their owner, Flat-Coated Retrievers are gentle, loving, kind and sweet. They are known for having a youthful outlook on life, often known for continuing puppy-like behaviour well into their adult life.

    Shauna tells us: "Flat Coated Retrievers are energetic and need a minimum of two hours of exercise a day, which can include long walks, playtime, and training. They enjoy running, swimming, scent games, and canine sports.

    "They will embrace training, as long as it is positive, reward-based, consistent, and engaging as they can get bored easily."

    Do Flat-Coated Retrievers have any health issues?

    "Flat Coated Retrievers are categorised as 'Category One' by the Kennel Club as they currently have no points of specific health concern. However, they do appear prone to certain cancers," Shauna says. "They will require daily brushing to maintain their coats, with particular attention to their leg feathers."

    Martin Sandera//Getty Images

    Do Flat-Coated Retrievers make good pets?

    Flat-Coated Retrievers are wonderful pets, particularly for those with young children. According to the PDSA, they are generally well behaved when left alone, as long as their exercise and training needs are met.

    "As with any dog, they shouldn't be unaccompanied for longer than four hours. This breed is best suited to a home with older children or teenagers, and, as with all dogs, they should always be supervised while around children," Shauna continues. "They can live with another dog quite happily if they are well socialised."

    What environment is best for a Flat-Coated Retriever?

    Although a Flat-Coated Retriever is fairly calm indoors, they will need a large secure garden to run around in. Unfortunately, due to their size, they are not suitable for people living in apartments.

    5 interesting facts about Flat-Coated Retrievers

  • They always have a positive outlook on life
  • They developed in England as a water dog and hunting companion in the 1800s, however lost popularity to Golden Retrievers
  • They are an incredibly hard-working breed that will always make you laugh
  • Flat-Coated Retrievers are considered large dogs, standing up to 24.5 inches tall
  • They enjoy running, swimming, scent games, and canine sports
