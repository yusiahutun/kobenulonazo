    A-listers can practically own anything they want, whether it’s a pair of ‘dirty’ shoes that cost so much or a $4,250 pendant necklace that’s suitable for stadium-wearing (okay, Taylor Swift!) There’s often no limit to what they have, which is why I pay extra attention to the brands celebs rely on. Sometimes, these labels are too far out of reach, like Meghan Markle’s $1,350 Chanel flats. Other times, they’re actually attainable for everyday people (aka, me), and Jennifer Lopez’s butt-lifting jeans fall into the latter.   

    Lopez was recently spotted out in New York City alongside Ben Affleck wearing a cozy turtleneck sweater under a structured blazer. She accessorized with high-end pieces like an Hermès Birkin Bag, Chloé sunglasses, Brunello Cucinelli scarf, and her 8.5-carat diamond ring, while sticking to ‘naked’ shoes from Andrea Wazen. But what really captured my attention were her wide-leg jeans. 

    Shop Lopez-Inspired Mother Denim Jeans

    This isn't the first time Lopez has worn Mother jeans, relying on the flattering brand as far back as 2014, with celebrities like Kate Middleton, Jennifer Garner, Meghan Markle, and Olivia Wilde slipping on the popular denim. Fashion editors have even put the label’s jeans to the test, too, saying they’re  “100 percent worth it” thanks to their durability, fit, and comfort. 

    Mother the Hustler Roller Sneak Flare Jeans

    Mother the Ditcher Roller Sneak Wide-Leg Jeans

    Unfortunately, Lopez’s exact style is almost sold out — but don’t worry! I found five lookalike options from Mother that are just as chic, like these Hustler Roller Sneak jeans. They have the same soft flare, worn-in knees, and wash; aside from that destructed hemline, they’re pretty spot on. 

    For a more dramatic effect, consider the Ditcher Roller Sneak jeans. Essentially, you’re trading the flared silhouette for a bigger, wider look (think: Katie Holmes), which is effortless and totally cool right now. Or, go the opposite route and opt for a slimmer take on Lopez’s butt-enhancing pants, as I know wide-leg jeans aren’t for everyone.  

    No matter which pair(s) you snag, you’ll be glad you did, especially when you have a large roster of A-listers and fashion editors backing them up. Just be sure to style them according to your own liking, whether that’s with a slinky tank and spring-approved wedges or a white tee and comfortable sneakers.
