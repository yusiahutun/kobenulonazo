    According to the 2023 Budget Policy Statement (BPS), the government will continue to monitor the global financial market conditions in light of the increased cost of financing.

    "The government will also explore other alternative sources of financing, including climate fund financing options, debt for nature swaps, Samurai and Panda bonds, depending on the prevailing market and macroeconomic conditions," the 2024 BPS stated.

    Bloomberg reported that Panda Bond's sale will take place in the 2024/2025 FY beginning July 1.

    Samurai Bond

    The planned Panda Bond sale came after Kenya established an agreement with Nippon Export and Investment Insurance (NEXI) to issue a Samurai Bond valued at KSh 66 billion.

    Nairobi will use proceeds from the Samurai Bond to promote electric vehicle usage and improve energy efficiency within Kenya's transmission network.

    FX Pesa lead market analyst Rufas Kamau warned that although the bond was good in the short term, Kenya could pay more if the Bank of Japan hikes interest rates.

    Why Kenya's debt will soar

    Kenya's National Treasury projected that the country's public debt will hit KSh 13.2 trillion by June 2027, up from KSh 10.3 trillion in June 2023.

    In its annual public debt management report for the 2022/2023 financial year, the exchequer noted domestic and external debt is expected to increase to KSh 6.82 trillion and KSh 6.4 trillion, respectively.

    However, the Treasury projects that the total public debt as a share of the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is expected to decline from 70.8% at the end of June 2023 to 57.7% at the end of June 2027.
