    A New York man will not face criminal charges in connection to the crash that killed his 22-year-old girlfriend, a recent Yale University graduate and promising writer whose final student newspaper column drew national attention.

    The Cape Cod Times reported that an Orleans District Court clerk magistrate ruled on Thursday that there was no probable cause to charge Michael Gocksch, of Centerport, New York, with negligent homicide with motor vehicle and reckless driving.

    According to the Yale Daily News, charges against Gocksch were dismissed without prejudice within an hour, a court clerk said Thursday afternoon.

    Cleared: Charges against Michael Gocksch, left, were dismissed in connection to the rollover death of his girlfriend Marina Keegan, right

    ‘I’m very happy for that young man because he’s a fine, fine person,’ Gocksch’s attorney, Philip Tracy Jr, told the station WHDH after Thursday’s ruling.

    The 22-year-old Gocksch was driving when Marina Keegan of Wayland, Massachusetts, died in the May 26 rollover on Route 6 in Dennis. The couple had graduated from Yale days earlier.

    State police said speed was not a factor, and no alcohol or drugs were found in Gocksch's system. Both he and Miss Keegan were wearing seat belts at the time of the crash.

    Keegan's father, Kevin Keegan, has previously claimed that Gocksch fell asleep at the wheel. However, on Thursday, Mr Keegan and his wife, Tracy, were by Gocksch's side in court when charges against him were dismissed.

    Tracy said the Keegans sent letters and made phone calls to state police asking them to not pursue charges against his client.

    ‘The thing about it, there was no speeding, no recklessness involved, no negligence,’ said Tracy. ‘He certainly wishes it was him and not her who suffered the fatality.

    The untimely death of Miss Keegan drew national attention because of her high-achieving past and future prospects.

    The column that she wrote for the Yale Daily News, encouraging young people to 'make something happen to this world', drew thousands of hits online after her death.

    Devastating loss: Marina Keegan, 22, died in a car crash in Cape Cod late last month

    Horrific: Miss Keegan died in a car crash while her boyfriend Mr Gocksch was driving

    The young couple was heading to Wellfleet, Massachusetts, on May 26 for a birthday dinner for Miss Keegan's father.

    Miss Keegan had been due to start at the famed New Yorker magazine this month as an editorial assistant and move into a Brooklyn apartment with friends.

    The 1997 black Lexus hit the guardrail before crossing to the opposite side of the road and rolling over twice. Miss Keegan died at the scene. She was apparently asleep in the passenger seat at the time.

    Her mother, Tracy Keegan, told the New York Daily News: 'She was just one of those amazing, wise souls that was given to us as a gift.

    'She had an unbelievable, beyond-her-years way of looking at the world, and her passion was to try and use her words to explore the human condition. This is one of her legacies that she will leave behind.'

    Dreams: The Yale graduate was an aspiring writer who was about to start work at The New Yorker magazine and move into a Brooklyn apartment with friends

    Full life: Marina Keegan wrote an article about how she had enjoyed her time being a student at Yale

    Miss Keegan studied English at Yale from 2008. She graduated magna cum laude five days before her death.

    The graduate was president of the Yale College Democrats and led Occupy Wall Street protests at the college.

    She had also written a musical called Independents which is due to run at New York International Fringe Festival in August.

    Her mother added that she had loved New York and couldn't wait to start her new life in the city.

    The student newspaper, the Yale Daily News, published one of the last articles that Miss Keegan had written before graduation, called The Opposite of Loneliness.

    In the article where she reflects on her hopes for life after college, she poignantly wrote: 'We’re so young. We’re so young. We’re twenty-two years old. We have so much time.'

    Miss Keegan is survived by her parents and brothers, Trevor and Pierce.

    Amazing: Miss Keegan was due to move into an apartment in Brooklyn with friends and start a job at The New Yorker magazine
