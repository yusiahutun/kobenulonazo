    Austin Kevitch Job

    Austin Kevitch is the founder of the dating app Lox Club which he launched in March 2020. He launched the app after a bad breakup and a bit of badgering from his bubbe.

    “I was at the age where my grandma would joke, ‘When are you going to bring home a nice Jewish girl?’” Austin told Bustle. While the members-only dating app is billed for “Jews with ridiculously high standards,” it’s really for anyone with a sense of humor — especially if it’s of the Larry David variety. “I don’t like dating apps; they’re very inorganic and awkward,” Austin said. “So I wanted to make a more fun, immersive experience where maybe you’ll meet people as a byproduct and not take it so seriously”.

    Austin had initially set up Lox Club as a joke several years ago before the demand prompted him to officially launch it in the fall of 2020. Speaking with E! News, he explained, “I wake up and over 1,000 people had applied on this website for an app that didn’t even exist”.

    “Now all of a sudden, everyone’s stuck at home, lonely and horny,” Austin said. “I’m getting all of these messages, like, ‘Hey, when am I going to get accepted to Lox Club?’ And then I’m thinking to myself, ‘All right, maybe it would make sense now with COVID. There’s no other real way for people to meet”.

    Austin sees the most growth potential for his company and wants his app to be the the “Disney of dating apps”.

    Austin also co-founded and served as the CEO of Brighten. The apps helped combat bullying. The app had one million users from high school and college. Meanwhile, he started out as a product designer at Scholly in 2011.
