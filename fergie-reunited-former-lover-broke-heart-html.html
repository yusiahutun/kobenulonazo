    Sarah, Duchess of York, has had a poignant reunion with her one-time lover Steve Wyatt at his family home.

    Fergie visited Steve and his parents in Houston, Texas, at a lunch to mark Mother’s Day in the U.S. 

    At the intimate occasion were Steve, his mother Lynn, his oil tycoon stepfather Oscar Wyatt, and Steve’s close friend, shoe designer Joyce Echols.

    Fergie, 57, met Steve in 1989 at Houston Grand Opera’s British Opera Festival, when she was already pregnant with her second child Princess Eugenie.

    Unwisely, Fergie formed a close association with Wyatt, who moved to London and squired the pregnant Duchess round the town, remarking at one dinner: ‘Mah woman sits next to me.’

    Fergie was soon infatuated. On finding out Wyatt had become engaged to Cate Magennis in 1993 she confided to a friend: ‘I can’t have the man I love because he’s getting married.

    ‘What’s the matter with me? Why didn’t he marry me?’

    When Wyatt was in London with his new wife, he took her to dinner at La Famiglia in Chelsea. Fergie got wind of his visit, drove to the restaurant and peered through the window in case she could see him. She didn’t and was too hesitant to go in.

    After their affair ended, she grew close to his pal John Bryan who famously sucked her toes while she sunbathed topless in the South of France.

    Bryan remained ‘fantastically jealous of Wyatt’.

    Fergie and Prince Andrew separated in 1992 and divorced in 1996. Wyatt is also amicably divorced from Cate, with whom he has two children.

    The highlight of last weekend’s lunch in River Oaks, Houston, was when Steve gave an emotional toast to his mother saying: ‘She gave me my heart.’ And Fergie, it seems, lost hers to him.

    Bowie's legacy? It's all b******s, blasts Angie 

    David Bowie's death in January last year seemed to provoke a state of national mourning, with his passing at the age of 69 after a drug-fuelled past leading BBC news bulletins.

    His former wife, Angie Bowie, refuses to join in the canonisation.

    ‘It’s a joke — just b******s,’ she fumes. ‘It’s like when people join a religion.

    ‘They get tunnel vision. He could never have been anything but perfect. They get on a roll and start thinking that he’s close to God, baby. No, he’s just another dude trying to make a buck.’

    Angie Bowie (pictured), who divorced the singer in 1980, refuses to join in the canonisation

    Angie, who divorced the singer in 1980, adds: ‘Do you want to hear something David did I never forgave him for?

    ‘Coming to Victoria station in a Mercedes Landau and giving a Hitler salute.

    ‘My father was a war hero. He was in the Philippines for three years, carried on a stretcher with malaria through the mountains of Northern Luzon — and you’re going to make a joke about England needing a fascist dictator to get back on its feet? The door closed that day, baby. I was done.

    ‘I don’t think we spent a night in the house together after that. I wouldn’t tolerate it.’
