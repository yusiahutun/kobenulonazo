13 Jul 2024, 00:00

Its pure liquid form is defined by absence – transparent, odourless, tasteless, near-colourless – yet it is the main constituent of the Earth’s hydrosphere and living organisms’ fluids. Water, water, everywhere. Etymologically, “water”, from the Old English wæter, came from the Proto-Germanic *watōr, ultimately descending from the Proto-Indo-European (PIE) *wód-r, a suffixed form of the PIE root *wed- “water, wet”. The PIE suffixed form *wód-r became *údōr “water” in the Hellenic language family, to become the Ancient Greek húdōr, and thence the Greek ydr(o) forms.
