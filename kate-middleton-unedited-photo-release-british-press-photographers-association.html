    Kate Middleton Faces Mounting Pressure to Release Unedited Photo

    02 Aug 2024, 00:00

    It’s been days since Kensington Palace released a digitally altered Mother’s Day portrait of Princess Kate with her three kids, but the controversy over the photo still hasn’t died down.

    In fact, demands for the royal family to release the original and unedited version of the portrait have only increased, despite the palace previously stating that they have no intention of doing so.

    On Monday, the British Press Photographers’ Association released a statement urging the royals “to make the original images available for inspection so that we can assess what has been done, make sure that it doesn’t happen again, and allow this story to be the last time that manipulated images are distributed to the media.”

    Related Stories

    News agencies including the Associated Press, Reuters, and Getty Images retracted distribution of the portrait on Monday after noticing the image had been excessively manipulated. The palace thereafter released a rare personal apology attributed to the Princess of Wales, who admitted to “occasionally experiment[ing] with editing” as an “amateur photographer.”

    “That should not be an end to the matter, however,” the BPPA said of Kate’s statement. “Whether the picture was manipulated to ‘look nice’ by someone who didn’t understand the importance of truth in images or whether there is something else behind their actions is not as important as getting the message out there that it is wrong.”

    After the photo was retracted, a palace source told Bazaar that Kate had made “made minor adjustments” to the picture and that there were no plans to release the original.

    The glaring Photoshop errors have fueled a growing furor over Kate’s absence from public-facing duties as she recovers from an unspecified abdominal surgery the palace announced she’d undergone in January. Viral social media conspiracies about the princess’s whereabouts have snowballed into international news stories, with reputable publications now analyzing the controversy and what it implies for the future of the monarchy.

    In addition to the BPPA, other members of the British press have called for the palace to release the unaltered portrait, too. Chris Ship, a royal editor for ITV News, posted on X, “The way to close everything down would be to release the original photo on which Kate decided to ‘experiment with editing’. That’s very unlikely, mind you ...”

    Read the BPPA’s entire statement below:

    The British Press Photographers’ Association applauds the actions of the major agencies who have had the courage to withdraw the recently distributed image showing Her Royal Highness The Princess of Wales and her children by issuing a “kill notice.” Their suspicions that the photograph had been digitally manipulated are well-founded and it is important for the reputation of press photography that they have acted as swiftly and decisively as they have.

    We are led to believe that HRH The Princess of Wales has said that she edited the image and has apologised. That should not be an end to the matter, however. Whether the picture was manipulated to “look nice” by someone who didn’t understand the importance of truth in images or whether there is something else behind their actions is not as important as getting the message out there that it is wrong.

    The BPPA has always spoken up in favour of making sure that the public has trust in the photographs produced by our members, as well as those who supply images to the news media. This has become more of an issue since pictures supplied by such a variety of sources have become more common and we would urge all of those sources to have a look at item 1, clause i) of the Editors’ Code of Practice which says:

    The press must take care not to publish inaccurate or misleading or distorted information or images, including headlines not supported by the text

    We would urge everyone involved to make the original images available for inspection so that we can assess what has been done, make sure that it doesn’t happen again, and allow this story to be the last time that manipulated images are distributed to the media.
