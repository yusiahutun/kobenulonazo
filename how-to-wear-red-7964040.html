    How to Wear Red, According to InStyle Editors

    02 Jun 2024, 00:00

    Provocative. Scintillating. Passionate. Red is a color that holds a lot of meaning in just about every facet of culture, fashion very much included. Cardi B's "Bodak Yellow" wouldn't be the same without her "bloody shoes," and this fall's fashion wouldn't be nearly as exciting without the signature shade.

    The Fall/Winter 2023 runways were alive with red in dozens of variations — scarlet, oxblood, carmine. Vivid red monochromatic looks were spotted at Stella McCartney, Elie Saab, Ferragamo, Blumarine, and Bottega Veneta. Meanwhile, Marc Jacobs chose lush reds to accent otherwise neutral looks. Why this shade in this moment? Fashion historian Ryan Yip has some theories.

    "Red is [usually] associated with seduction and sexiness, but I think this year's trend's usage is to give vitality or a reminder of vitality, like a wake-up call to remind us that we're still alive," Yip tells InStyle. He posits: "Could it be that red is a type of spiritual armor to help us go through political and economic turmoil?"

    Regardless of the reason for its renaissance, red is undeniably a power color. Set against the backdrop of the chainmail and battle references seen on recent runways — Jean Paul Gaultier's Fall/Winter 2023 Haute Couture collection, rife with armor worthy of Joan of Arc herself, comes to mind — Yip's theory tracks. "Fall 2023's red fixation combines hope and masking," Yip suggests. "It's indicating vigor with hints of protection and a bit of 'fake it till you make it.'"

    With so many designers painting their runways red, we've been eyeing the color for our real-life wardrobes. Ahead, you'll find red clothing and accessories, large and small, that InStyle editors are lusting after this fall. From rosy red dresses to burgundy sunglasses, these editors' picks will get you warmed up in no time.

    Manolo Blahnik Maysale 70 Buckled Suede Mules

    To Shop: $795, Manolo Blahnik
    "Was I influenced by Hailey Bieber’s human-strawberry parade a few weeks back? Yes. Are the berry red Maysale Manolos she wore unequivocally, show-stoppingly perfect? Also yes. Do I need these reddiest of red mules? Most definitely, yes. Thanks to the on-trend hue, I have an excuse to buy these heels right now, but because of their classic style, they’ll be a wardrobe staple in my closet for seasons to come." —Sally Holmes, Editor-in-Chief

    BDG Mandi Denim Maxi Skirt

    To Shop: $69, Urban Outfitters
    "Denim maxiskirts are my obsession for fall, but in a sea of basic blues, I want to stand out in a more eye-catching iteration of one of the year’s biggest fads. Enter Urban Outfitters and their trusty BDG denim line. The Mandi Denim Maxi Skirt is the training wheels of long denim skirts, thanks to its leg slit and soft, comfortable denim. Try the deep red shade for a cool-girl punk (and autumnal) take on a style that’s been littering the streets and subways of New York City all summer long." —Tess Petak, Staff Writer

    J.Crew Cashmere V-Neck Sweater Vest

    To Shop: $98, J. Crew
    "When J.Crew’s new fall collection dropped, the first thing that caught my eye was this sweater vest. I love a clothing item that helps make the dance between summer and fall in New York just a little bit easier. Its bright color goes with a wide array of my basics, and I’m sizing it up so that I can layer it all throughout the year." —Jenna Brillhart, Creative Director

    Away Travel "The Large" Suitcase

    To Shop: $435, Away Travel
    "Away completely revamped its lineup for the first time in seven years. Not only is there a new trunk-shaped silhouette, but the beloved luggage company also redesigned the interior of its suitcases, offering even more organization for anyone whose passion is packing (me, I'm anyone). Of course, the red on offer is a deep and sophisticated glossy Bordeaux that they officially call garnet, and which will stand out at the luggage carousel in a sea of gray and black. My favorite part is that Away added a new handle on the bottom so that you don't have to do the baggage claim shuffle when your suitcase inevitably flips and flops on its way around." —Christopher Luu, Senior News Editor

    Réalisation Par Winoa Cardigan

    To Shop: $120, Réalisation Par
    "You can’t beat a classic cardigan in terms of versatility. This Jackie O-inspired silhouette is my favorite for all-season layering (I keep a black one in my purse to combat office A/C), and I’m currently thinking about adding a red version to my rotation. This ruby-colored cardigan from Réalisation Par is the ideal crop and fitted to perfection." —Madeline Hirsch, News Director

    To Shop: $825, Ganni

The contrasting off-white and cherry leather mean they go with double the outfits — a must when factoring in cost-per-wear. They’re an investment, to be sure, but cowboy boots never go out of style, so I predict this purchase will pay off in spades." —Lindy Segal, Interim Fashion Director

    Babaà Cardigan No3

I love the checkerboard knit, which takes your regular cardigan and turns it on its head. It’s important to me that my investment pieces make a statement, which makes this cardigan a perfect addition to my wardrobe." —Kelly Chiello, Associate Photo Director

With an already overly stuffed work tote for my daily treks to the office, a belt bag secures my most needed and valuable items in an easily accessible place. This little number from Longchamp is on my list for fall accessories this season. It’s both sophisticated and casual, and its bold color will pop in the shuffle of my daily schlep." —J.B.

The green-striped option was my summer go-to (thanks to HoYeun saying they were her preferred color), but I’ll swap them out for the red stripes as soon as these land on my porch. They really do go with everything and the hint of red will keep them distinct from everyone else’s black-and-white pairs." —C.L.

    To Shop: $268, Cuyana

The Cuyana Easy Tote feels like the perfect big-girl bag to upgrade my whole commuting uniform and the coral shade is perfect to match my wardrobe year-round." —K.C.

I love a moody color palette for fall and these classic over-the-knee Stuart Weitzman boots fit seamlessly into my cool weather uniform of oversized sweaters, miniskirts, and tights. The cut of the boot combined with its low heel means I don’t have to choose between being comfortable and feeling marvelously sophisticated. The gold hardware adds a unique element to my sunglass collection and spices up outfits that, because of the same jackets and boots, can start to feel repetitive." —Amanda Lauro, Graphic Designer

A rope-top handle makes it a true bucket bag while a detachable leather strap allows you to easily transform the statement piece into a functional crossbody.
