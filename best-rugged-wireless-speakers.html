    FAQ

    Q: Can You Leave Wireless Speakers Exposed Outside During Winter?

    While rugged speakers are designed to take abuse, the cold temperatures of winter might be too much for them. If the temperature becomes too low, it could negatively affect the internal battery of your wireless device.

    Furthermore, moisture inside your device could freeze and cause frost to form inside, damaging its sensitive electronics.

    Q: Can Too Much Bass Damage Speakers?

    When you increase the bass beyond your speaker's capabilities, you risk damaging its speaker cones. This is called over-excursion, where the cones in the speaker deform and eventually break.

    Q: How Waterproof Is a Waterproof Speaker?

    If you want confidence that your speaker won't get damaged when submerged in water, you should look for its IP rating. IP, or Ingress Protection, is an international standard that defines the effectiveness of an electrical device's external casing in keeping the elements out.

    You will find two digits following the letters IP. For example, IP67. The first digit refers to the device's protection against intrusion (dustproof), while the second digit refers to its moisture protection (waterproof). Any device with a rating of five means that it's protected from light rain, while a rating of six certifies it for protection against heavy rain.

    A rating of seven means you can submerge your gadget by up to 1m for up to 30 minutes. A rating of 8 or 9 means that the device is fully protected against water ingress, allowing you to use it in deeper water. However, you have to consult with the manufacturer to determine the exact specifications of your speaker if you have this rating.
