    Eric Church fans are closer to having a piece of the singer in their homes.

    The "Record Year" singer's Highway to Home furniture line is now available at select retailers. Inspired by Church's love of the road and family traditions, his collection includes bedroom, dining room, upholstery and accent pieces for every home.

    Church's first job was in a furniture warehouse, and his new line combines the singer's music and lifestyle. "With a foundation of family values, love of nature and a dash of attitude, Highway to Home is simply music for the home," the line's website states.

    “Staying connected with my roots has always been important to me and Highway to Home is a natural extension of my family heritage,” Church says in a press release. “My dad was in the industry. My first job was in a furniture plant, so yes, it is in my blood. This project is truly about building a home around those experiences and memories that make up our lives.”

    Highway to Home features four collections: Heartland Falls, Silverton Sound, Arrow Ridge and Hickory Canyon. The collections range from a "nostalgic mix of arts and crafts to urban-industrial to eclectic, all with a touch of rock ‘n’ roll spirit."

    “Highway to Home is about the journey home — the ultimate destination,” says Pulaski Furniture President Page Wilson, Church’s partner in the venture. “This is not necessarily about Eric’s songs or performances, but instead founded on the adventures and experiences of a traveling musician through the eyes of a creative writer as he travels the world from show to show and home again.”

    A portion of the proceeds from Highway to Home will be donated to the Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation. For more information on the furniture line, visit Church's website.
