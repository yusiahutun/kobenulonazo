    The incident occurred in August, yards from the entrance of Concert Square's Einstein Bier Haus, a packed beer tent.

    At the time, Liverpool mayor Joanne Anderson slammed the behaviour as "damaging and criminal"

    Firby, 23, and Cousins met each other in the gym before going out for a heavy boozy session, which resulted in them getting overly intimate to the shock of onlookers and thousands of social media users.

    Merseyside police were alerted to the incident and led to both parties being charged.

    Firby from Gateshead, Tyne and Wear, had been dodging appearing at Liverpool Magistrates Court due to being unable to afford the travel, having lost his job.

    On Wednesday, however, Firby finally made the trip and pled guilty to committing an act of outraging public decency. He is due to be sentenced in the new year.

    Cousins, who also pled guilty to the offence, was recently given a 10 week prison sentence, suspended for 12 months, and ordered to complete 40 hours of unpaid work and 25 Rehabilitation Activity days with the Probation Service.

    “We went on a date. Things got out of hand, this was never planned.

    “I deeply regret it - I have always taken responsibility for my actions and I now just want to make a new start and put this all behind me.”

    She said: “As we have heard, there was no statement made by any member of the public, nobody made a complaint about this.

    "It was the circulation of the video that alerted the police., and of course there was no victim impact statement. In fact, it can be seen that people are filming this willingly; laughing, jeering it on."
