    The beauty, fashion, and modeling industries are huge across the globe. Modeling agencies scout for models in different ways. Every top model featured in glamorous magazines, and a big fashion house has a story to tell. Many African models have done the continent proud by beating all odds and representing Africa on the global stage. The top African models we see on runways in New York, Paris, Venice, Sydney, and other cities still maintain their African roots. Even in foreign countries, African female models never forget to display their African side as they create their unique styles.

    Modeling was taken as a serious profession in the 1940s. Ford Models that was founded by Eileen and Gerard Ford in 1946 is considered one of the very first modeling agencies to be created. Modeling came to Africa in later years due to various factors. It is not every model who gets the chance to work for renowned fashion houses like Gucci, Prada, Calvin Klein, Christian Dior, and Chanel. Models that get to walk runways for known designers make it big because of the platform the fashion houses have. Being an African model and getting to work for top brands is a dream come true for many.

Some of the top models from Africa have had to undergo many challenges before their success. Some models have had to flee their countries for good, because of political instability and civil wars.     1. Alek Wek

    Standing at 5'11", Alek Wek is considered one of the most successful models from Sudan. Alek is of the Dinka tribe in Sudan. Alek's start to the journey to stardom was thanks to a models scout who noticed her in 1995 at an outdoor market in Crystal Palace, London. The Sudanese-British beauty who was born on 16th April 1977 started her modeling career and got signed to Ford Models in 1996. Alek is the first African model to appear on the cover of Elle. She graced the magazine in 1997, the same year she was declared Model of the Year by MTV. The 42-year-old has modeled for Christian Dior, Ralph Lauren, Tommy Hilfiger, Dolce & Gabbana, Moschino and Givenchy among many other top fashion houses.

    2. Maria Borges

    The Victoria's Secret model started her career in 2010. Maria Borges was born in Luanda, Angola on 28th October 1992. She stands at 5'11". Maria signed with Supreme Agency in 2012 and participated in her first fashion week a month later. The Angolan beauty walked 17 runways on her debut fashion week. Maria's 2017 Elle cover was iconic as she is the second African supermodel to appear on the cover. The first was Alek Wek in 1997. She has been covered by other popular magazines like Harper's Bazaar, Marie Claire, Numèro, Vogue and Interview. The 26-year-old has walked the runways for Giorgio Armani, Oscar de la Renta, Versace, Marc Jacobs, Zac Posen, and the Banana Republic among others.

The South African supermodel has walked the runway for Jean Paul Gaultier, Christian Dior, Diane von Fürstenberg, Sportmax, Viktor and Rolf, Chanel and Fendi. The 30-year-old who stands 5'10" high is hailed as one of the most successful South African female models.
