    Philip Green's wife snaps up a 10MILLION London townhouse

    Herhusband may be fighting to save his reputation, but Tina Green's spending shows no sign of slowing down. Sir Philip Green's 67-year-old wife is understood to be behind a company that has spent more than 10million on a London townhouse, which is now being renovated under her watchful eye.

    22 May 2024, 00:00

    Her husband may be fighting to save his reputation, but Tina Green's spending shows no sign of slowing down.

    Sir Philip Green's 67-year-old wife is understood to be behind a company that has spent more than £10million on a London townhouse, which is now being renovated under her watchful eye.

    The news will no doubt anger BHS staff who lost their jobs and still face uncertainty over pensions following the collapse of the retail chain.

    Sir Philip Green's 67-year-old wife is understood to be behind a company that has spent more than £10million on a London townhouse (pictured)

    The Green family appear to have gone to great lengths to conceal their investment in the home, which has six bedrooms and a roof terrace (Sir Philip after being knighted in 2006)

    It went into administration after Sir Philip – known as Sir Shifty – sold it for £1 to serial bankrupt Dominic Chappell, with a £571million pension scheme deficit. 

    Lady Green, who controls the family fortune and is worth £5billion, was in London on Tuesday to visit the property. 

    Wearing all black, she was later whisked away in a Mercedes with darkened windows. 

    The Green family appear to have gone to great lengths to conceal their investment in the home, which has six bedrooms and a roof terrace.

    The four-storey Belgravia townhouse was sold to Daniel Pittack, a close friend of the Greens, in November 2014 for more than £10million.

    Then, last December, ownership was transferred to Mottistone Holdings Ltd, a newly formed firm based in the British Virgin Islands, for around £100,000 more than Mr Pittack paid.

    A second address for the offshore company was in Boulevard Princesse Charlotte in Monaco – also the registered office for Lady Green's interior design firm, Green and Mingarelli Design.

    There have been a number of planning applications for renovations at the house since October 2014, including for a new roof terrace, lowering the basement floor, and installation of a glass roof. 

    The Green family appear to have gone to great lengths to conceal their investment in the home, which has six bedrooms and a roof terrace

    Sir Philip Green's £100million super yacht, Lionheart in Valletta, Malta earlier this year

    Lady Green herself received hundreds of millions of pounds in dividends from BHS before it was sold and lives in Monaco, a tax haven

    Asked about the house, Sir Philip said: 'Let's be clear, I don't know anything about it. What is the relevance anyway?

    'I am not her [Lady Green]. I have no idea. For God's sake, can't you people just get lost, can't you go and annoy somebody else?'

    The use of an off-shore company is legitimate but controversial as they are often used to minimise tax.

    A spokesman for Sir Philip and Lady Green said Lady Green has not received any money from BHS for more than 10 years but the apparent purchase is likely to irritate former BHS workers, 22,000 of which risk losing part of their pension.

    Sir Philip was accused of 'systematically' plundering from the company and enriching his family with dividend cheques, before leading it to disaster prompting calls for him to be stripped of his knighthood.

    Lady Green herself received hundreds of millions of pounds in dividends from BHS before it was sold and lives in Monaco, a tax haven.

    Last month, she was seen taking a private helicopter to travel just 13 miles from Monte Carlo in Monaco to Nice in France - a journey would have taken half an hour by car.

    A week earlier, she was seen boarding the helicopter last week with a handbag thought to be worth £27,000 - the same as the average Briton's annual salary. 

    Sir Philip was accused of 'systematically' plundering from BHS and enriching his family with dividend cheques

    She paired the bag, believed to be by Chanel and made of alligator skin, with large diamond earrings and a huge jewel on her finger.

    Earlier this year, the couple took delivery of their new £100 million superyacht, called Lionheart which is the length of a football pitch and has a helipad on its upper deck.

    The couple also own the 109-foot superyacht Lionchase, which is used mostly by their two grown-up children, Chloe and Brandon.

    Sir Philip told MPs more than two months ago that he would 'sort' the pensions' crisis.

    He is said to have offered to stump up £250million but the figure is still £100million less than the Pensions Regulator demanded and there are fears the legal battle could drag on for a decade.

    Regulators have taken the rare step of launching a legal claim to force him to plug the black hole but experts fear the dispute could take years to resolve because the retail tycoon and the regulator have failed to agree how much he should pay.
