    The Duchess of Sussex recently enjoyed some quality time with friends by hitting the slopes.

    Duchess Meghan embarked on a ski trip with a couple of her longtime girlfriends: Heather Dorak, owner of workout studio franchise Pilates Platinum, and Kelly McKee Zajfen, co-founder of philanthropist group Alliance of Moms.

    In a photo Dorak posted on Instagram, the trio posed together for a smiley picture, surrounded by snow-covered mountains. Meghan wore a black monochromatic look, wearing a long, partially zipped hooded puffer coat from Aritzia, snow pants, a ribbed knit beanie, large sunglasses, and gloves. Her pals were similarly bundled up for the cold temps in their own puffers and knit hats.

    “Wow, what a perfect trip!! My heart is so full,” Dorak captioned the post. “The kids crushed the slopes by day and the adults howled with laughter deep into the night. Truly lucky in love with our wonderful friends!”

    Other snaps included in the same post capture sweet moments with Dorak’s family, as well as Dorak and McKee Zajfen heading down a slope.

    Meghan embarked on another snowy trip in mid-February—this one to Canada, with husband Prince Harry. The three-day visit took them through Vancouver and Whistler, in the westernmost province of British Columbia, where they met with athletic competitors and coaches and kicked off the countdown to the 2025 Invictus Games.

    The Vancouver Whistler games—scheduled for February 8 to 16, 2025—will be the first winter sporting event in Invictus history. More than 500 athletes from 20 countries—injured in some way while serving in the armed forces—are expected to compete in alpine skiing, Nordic skiing, skeleton, wheelchair curling, indoor rowing, sitting volleyball, swimming, wheelchair basketball, and wheelchair rugby.
